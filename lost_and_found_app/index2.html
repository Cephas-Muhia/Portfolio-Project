Lost & Found App

"A customizable web app that helps users report and locate ​lost items, offering seamless integration with existing ​systems for a user-friendly experience."



4o


Login window

The login window in the Lost & Found App allows users to ​securely access their accounts using their email and ​password. Additionally, it offers the convenience of social ​logins through Facebook and Google, enabling users to ​quickly sign in without creating a new account. This ensures a ​seamless and user-friendly experience, allowing users to ​report or search for lost items efficiently.

Reporting Lost Item window

This window in the Lost & Found App allows users to submit ​detailed information about their lost items, including ​descriptions, location, date of loss, and photos. This ​information is then made available in the app’s database, ​enabling other users to view and help locate the reported ​items. The window ensures that all necessary details are ​captured to maximize the chances of recovering the lost item.

Search window

The search result window in the Lost & Found App displays ​a list of items matching the user's search criteria, such as ​descriptions, locations, and dates. Each result includes key ​details and photos of the found items, allowing users to ​quickly identify potential matches. The window also ​provides contact options for users to communicate with ​the person who reported the found item, facilitating the ​recovery process.

About section

The inspiration for creating the Lost & Found App came from ​a personal experience. A few years ago, I lost a cherished ​item during a family vacation and struggled to recover it due ​to a lack of efficient lost and found services. This frustration ​highlighted the need for a centralized platform to report and ​track lost items. Determined to address this gap, I ​developed the Lost & Found App as my Portfolio Project for ​Holberton School. Over several months, I designed and ​refined the app to ensure a user-friendly interface and ​seamless integration with existing systems. The app enables ​users to report lost items, view reported items, and ​communicate with others, making the search process more ​efficient and less stressful. You can follow my journey and ​see the development process on LinkedIn, explore the ​project on GitHub, or connect with me on Twitter for ​updates and insights.
